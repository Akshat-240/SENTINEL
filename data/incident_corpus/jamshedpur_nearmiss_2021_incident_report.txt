INCIDENT REPORT
Title: Confined Space Gas Near-Miss, Jamshedpur
Date: 2021
Location: Jamshedpur, Jharkhand
Type: Near-miss - gas accumulation in confined space, no fatalities

SUMMARY:
During a routine confined space entry (combined with an active electrical
work permit in the same zone), workers noticed a gradual, moderate rise in
gas readings on a handheld detector. The shift supervisor, acting on
experience and vigilance rather than any automated alert, ordered an
immediate evacuation. Follow-up investigation confirmed gas levels had been
approaching the danger threshold and would likely have caused a serious
incident within roughly two more hours if unaddressed.

KEY FACTORS:
- Gas trend: rising moderately over an extended period (slower than the
  Visakhapatnam and Haldia cases)
- Active permits: Confined Space + Electrical (same zone)
- Shift: Night shift
- Worker count in affected zone: 3
- Zone risk history: Moderate

OUTCOME:
No injuries or fatalities. Evacuation completed with an estimated 2-hour
safety margin before the situation would have become critical.

ROOT CAUSE:
Absence of automated gas monitoring integrated with the confined space
permit system; safety outcome depended entirely on individual worker
vigilance rather than a systemic safeguard.

LESSONS LEARNED:
1. Manual vigilance is not a reliable substitute for automated, continuous
   monitoring - this incident succeeded only because of an experienced
   supervisor's judgment call.
2. Systems that flag "slow moderate rise" trends (not just sharp spikes)
   are valuable, since slower near-misses like this one can still become
   fatal without early detection.
3. This case is a useful contrast to Visakhapatnam/Haldia: same category of
   hazard (gas + permit overlap), very different outcome, due to earlier
   human intervention.
