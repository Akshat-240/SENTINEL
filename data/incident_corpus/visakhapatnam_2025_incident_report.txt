INCIDENT REPORT
Title: Styrene Gas Leak at Chemical Storage Facility, Visakhapatnam
Date: January 2025
Location: Industrial zone, Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh
Type: Toxic gas leak (confined space + hot work overlap)

SUMMARY:
A styrene vapor leak occurred during night shift operations at a chemical
storage tank. Investigation found that a Hot Work permit for repair welding
was issued in the same zone where a Confined Space entry permit was already
active, without cross-checking live gas sensor readings. Gas concentration
rose from approximately 20 PPM to over 800 PPM within 10 minutes. Workers in
the vicinity were not evacuated before the threshold breach because the
permit system and the sensor monitoring system operated independently of
each other, with no automatic linkage or compound-risk alert.

KEY FACTORS:
- Gas trend: sharp exponential rise within a 10-minute window
- Active permits: Hot Work + Confined Space (same zone, same time)
- Shift: Night shift, reduced supervisory staffing
- Worker count in affected zone: 8
- Zone risk history: High (prior minor leaks reported in preceding 2 years)

OUTCOME:
Multiple fatalities and injuries. Emergency shutdown was triggered manually,
after the safe intervention window had already closed.

ROOT CAUSE:
Lack of real-time correlation between active work permits and live sensor
trends. No automated compound-risk scoring existed to flag the dangerous
overlap of Hot Work + Confined Space entry during rising gas conditions.

LESSONS LEARNED:
1. Permit issuance systems must be cross-checked against live sensor data
   before approval, not just at the time of manual review.
2. Compound risk (multiple simultaneous hazardous permits in one zone)
   should trigger an automatic elevated risk score.
3. Night shifts require the same or stricter automated safety oversight
   as day shifts, since supervisory presence is typically lower.
