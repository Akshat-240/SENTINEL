INCIDENT REPORT
Title: Hot Work Fire Incident, Haldia Refinery Area
Date: 2019
Location: Haldia, West Bengal
Type: Fire following hot work near flammable vapor accumulation

SUMMARY:
A fire broke out during a hot work (welding) operation carried out under an
approved Hot Work permit. Gas monitoring later showed flammable vapor
concentration had been rising steadily in the hours before the permit was
approved, but the permit approval process did not incorporate a live check
of ambient gas levels at the work location. The fire spread quickly due to
the presence of flammable material within the safety radius of the hot work.

KEY FACTORS:
- Gas trend: rising sharply in the hours before ignition
- Temperature trend: stable, not a leading indicator in this case
- Active permit: Hot Work only
- Shift: Day shift
- Worker count in affected zone: 5

OUTCOME:
Fire caused multiple fatalities and significant equipment damage. Cause was
traced to inadequate gas-level verification prior to permit approval.

ROOT CAUSE:
Hot work permits were approved based on a checklist review rather than a
live, automated safety-threshold check against current sensor readings.

LESSONS LEARNED:
1. Hot Work permits should not be approved when gas readings exceed a
   defined safety threshold, verified automatically at approval time.
2. Permit systems should re-validate conditions periodically during long
   duration hot work jobs, not just once at issuance.
